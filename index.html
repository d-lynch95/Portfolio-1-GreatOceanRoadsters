Attractions

Kennet Koala Walk

One of the best places in Australia to spot koalas in their natural surroundings. Grab a coffee at Koala Cafe and take a walk up the old river road to spot the koalas in the trees.

Twelve Apostles

An absolute must on your visit to the Great Ocean Road. Bask in the views of these amazing sea stacks. Make sure you don't forget your camera!

Hopkins Falls

Measuring 90 metres wide and plunging 12 metres over dark basalt rocks, the curtain-like hopkins falls are a must see on your road trip.

Catch a wave

With over 200km of coastline you're spoiled for choice in this surfers paradise. Either find a secluded cove or hang 10 at one of the busier beaches.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Great Ocean Road?

The Great Ocean Road is an Australian National Heritage listed 240-kilometre stretch of road along the south-eastern coast of Australia between the Victorian cities of Torquay and Allansford.

What is the best way to travel the Great Ocean Road?

The best way to see the great ocean road is by car or if you wish to prolong your visit you can travel in a motorhome or caravan. You can also take a motorbike and some people have been known to walk the route. The Great Ocean Road is also the site for an annual marathon and cycling race.

What are the main attractions?

There are many attractions along the great open road and we reccomend you visit our Itinerary page or you can contact us for further information.

When is the best time to visit?

You can visit the Great Ocean Road at any time of the year but we reccomend you visit in summer to take advantage of the amazing Australian weather.
